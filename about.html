About me.

After receiving a Bachelor's degree in computer science at Northeastern Illinois University, I was hired by Allstate Insurance to work in Life systems. My tasks included:

  • Project lead, Requirements Definition, Analysis and design
  • Debugging & Troubleshooting
  • Testing & Documentation
  • New employee and User Training & Support
  • Policy exhibit and valuation department
  • Billing and Collections - worked on first electronic funds transfer system with Chase Manhattan Bank
  • Programed with COBOL and Assembler on an IBM 370 mainframe

While working on my first bachelor's degree in computer science, I was doing a lot of landscape photography, team pictures and even wedding photography. I did not just program at Allstate either. I started training under a professional chef in Chicago, Cleatis Wilcox, the first certified female chef in the Midwest. Cooking must be in my blood too! My family includes a chef who worked at the American Embassy in Oslo, Norway! After marrying I moved to Michigan where I cooked professionally and also did IT consulting work.

My roles as a chef included:

  • Baker
  • Line Cook in a resort area
  • Prep Cook
  • Sous Chef for a retirement home (Kitchen manager)
  • Recipes developed for special dietary needs.

Computers were never far from my mind either. I worked on storage methods for my vast library of recipes. I createdd ways to calculate portions and nutritional values because of special dietary needs. A lot of my work was done with Visual Basic, stand-alone apps through Excel macros. In the kitchen I worked on inventory, scheduling and ordering systems. Other consulting work I did was set up a network for a medical practice and a database for a local horticultural therapy farm where I was their volunteer coordinator.

My volunteer activities included:
